Detailed Description

The Bond class holds the information of a bond.

A bond is defined from the quantum states specified by quantum numbers defined by the Qnum class. For example, a bond representing the states of a spin-1 particle with local basis states of \(|-1\rangle, |0\rangle, |1\rangle\). In this case, the dimension of the bond is three. Each state of the bond carries a Qnum, which is the eigenvalue of the symmetry operators.

For example, For a spin system with \(U(1)\) symmetry which conserves the total \(S_z\), we can define a bond with three distinct Qnum with \(S_z= -1, 0, 1\).
